Educational Tour — Cebu & Bohol

Day 1

Worldtech Information Solutions Inc.

Observations: Worldtech is a well-established IT consultancy and training provider with a strong presence in Cebu, Manila, and beyond. They specialize in cybersecurity training and offer various certification programs, with highly experienced trainers.

Learnings: Worldtech’s focus on practical, hands-on training ensures immediate application of skills in real-world jobs. Their strong emphasis on cybersecurity and operational tech protection highlights the importance for organizations to stay secure and up-to-date.

Day 2

Codechum and Rivan IT

First stop: Codechum

Observations: Codechum is a highly user-friendly platform that makes learning to code easier and more engaging. It automates the code-checking process, supporting various programming languages, and is useful for both students and teachers. I noticed its mobile-friendly design and AI-powered study tools make it convenient and modern.

Learnings: Codechum helps improve coding skills by providing instant feedback and guided practice. It also supports teachers by simplifying monitoring and reducing cheating.

Second stop: Rivan IT

Observations: Rivan IT Cebu’s training center is very organized and professional. Their courses are well-maintained, and the instructors are highly knowledgeable. The center provides a great environment for students, especially in networking and IT certifications such as Cisco and Linux, which are crucial for their career training.

Learnings: I learned that Rivan IT Cebu offers a strong environment to build practical IT and networking skills. They focus on preparing students for real-world IT roles and help them gain confidence and competence in the field.

Day 3

MATA Technologies, Inc.

Observations: During my visit to MATA Technologies, Inc., I was impressed by their optics and innovation in the virtual reality and technology sector. Their team is friendly and passionate about their work, showcasing how they are creating interactive virtual tours for real estate and tourism. I noticed how advanced their platform helps clients deliver “smarter” tours — opening new possibilities for customers.

Learnings: I learned that MATA Technologies is bridging tourism and technology by bringing immersive virtual reality experiences to the Philippines, which helps make real estate and tourism more interactive and accessible. Overall, their work showed how virtual reality can have a big impact in both tourism and real estate, providing sustainable solutions for the future.

Day 4

Telephone and Radio System Integrated Emergency Response (TaRSIER 117)

Observations: During my visit to TaRSIER 117, I observed that their mobile unit is well-organized and equipped for quick emergency response. The team is highly professional, approachable, and clearly dedicated to ensuring the safety of both locals and tourists. I also noticed strong collaboration with other agencies like the PNP, BFP, and DOT, which shows how seriously they take public safety and disaster preparedness.

Learnings: I learned that TaRSIER 117 plays a crucial role in keeping the community safe and ready for emergencies. I realized the importance of teamwork, training, and proper equipment in effective disaster management. Overall, my visit showed me that having a dedicated and well-prepared response team can greatly enhance the safety and confidence of both residents and visitors.
